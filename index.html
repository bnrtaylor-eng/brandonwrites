About Me

I am an Assistant Professor of English at Mount Marty University, where I specialize in early modern literature and political theology. My research examines how literary and rhetorical forms shaped—and were shaped by—the institutional and economic structures of the seventeenth century. I am also developing a TEI-based digital edition of The Letters of John Chamberlain, an open-access archive of early modern correspondence. This work sits at the crossroads of literary history, political theology, and the longer history of capitalism.

Currently (March 2026)

  • Revising Empire of the Unincorporated for submission with a university press.
  • Developing course materials for News Writing and Adolescent/Young Adult Literature courses.
  • Building contributor tools for Early Modern Letters.

This site is a record of my work as a scholar, teacher, and editor—including the ongoing development of the Early Modern Letters project and collaborative work with students.
